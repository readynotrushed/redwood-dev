05 — The reasons

The problem is not engagement. It's logistics.

Children want to connect with their teachers and peers. Parents are asking for flexibility to make it happen.

Time

Only one or two timing options

Time zones, toddler nap schedules, siblings with overlapping Zoom calls. Fixed morning sessions exclude a significant portion of families who genuinely cannot make it work. These families are not disengaged. They are just on a different clock.

Attention

Screen fatigue has created a natural split

Some children can still thrive in a structured online class. Others have stopped showing up entirely. For those who have checked out of academic online learning, the opportunity is to meet them on a social and emotional level instead.

Commitment

30 minutes is a barrier for some

Parents have said it directly: a 30 minute class is too much of a commitment on a hard day. The same families would join a shorter, informal drop-in session. The format is the barrier, not the will.
